The 1TB Seagate Barracuda 2016 (ST1000DM010) has an impressive
performance profile. With Sequential read/writes averaging 173 and 159
MBps respectively, the Barracuda can make short work of even
moderately large backups. The small file (4K) performance profile is
less impressive but still adequate with average read/writes coming in at
0.87 and 1.53 MBps respectively. For use as OS drives, rotational disks
are quickly loosing market share to SSDs which offer orders of
magnitude faster 4k read/write speeds. On the other hand cheap TLC
based SSDs often have slower sustained write speeds than their
rotational counterparts. Reasonably good overall performance can be
achieved by using a TLC SSD to host the OS and a larger rotational
drive such as the Barracuda for backups and media files. Larger
capacity variants of this drive offer both better performance and better
value for money.
